Getting LinkTopics running with your LinkedIn profile doesn’t require any technical skills. It behaves like any other Chrome extension — the difference is what it does to your feed once it’s installed.

Getting Started With Installation

Starting with LinkTopics takes less than a minute. If you’ve ever added a browser extension before, this will feel familiar:

  1. Open the LinkTopics page on the Chrome Web Store.
  2. Click “Add to Chrome” and confirm the installation.
  3. Pin the LinkTopics icon in your browser toolbar so it’s easy to access.
  4. Open linkedin.com in a new tab — LinkTopics will start analysing the feed immediately.

If nothing appears to change, double-check that the extension is enabled and that you’re actually on LinkedIn (LinkTopics only runs on linkedin.com).

Frequently Asked Questions

What is LinkTopics and how does it help on LinkedIn?

LinkTopics is a Chrome extension that cleans your LinkedIn feed. It hides ads, promoted posts, “Liked by” clutter and suggested posts so you see more of the content that actually matters to your work.

Is LinkTopics a LinkedIn ad blocker?

LinkTopics works in a similar way to a LinkedIn adblocker, but goes further. It doesn’t just hide many promoted posts and sponsored updates — it also reduces “Liked by” posts, suggested posts and other noisy content types that generic adblockers usually miss.

How do I start using LinkTopics with my LinkedIn account?

Install LinkTopics from the Chrome Web Store, pin the extension icon and then open linkedin.com in your browser. The extension automatically starts analysing and cleaning your feed; you can then adjust filters and topics inside the LinkTopics popup.

Can I control which posts LinkTopics hides?

Yes. You can enable or disable filters for ads, promoted posts, “Liked by” posts and suggested posts, and you can also choose topics you want to see more of. This gives you control over how aggressive the filtering should be.

Does LinkTopics store my LinkedIn data?

LinkTopics processes your feed locally in your browser. It analyses post structure and text to decide what to hide, but it is not a data-selling tool — it exists purely to give you a calmer, more focused LinkedIn experience.

Can teams use LinkTopics together?

Yes. Each team member installs LinkTopics individually, but you can share recommended settings and topics so everyone has a similar, clean LinkedIn experience. This makes it easier for sales, recruiting or leadership teams to stay aligned while using LinkedIn every day.
