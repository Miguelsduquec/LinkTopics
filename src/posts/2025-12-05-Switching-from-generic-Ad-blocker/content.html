Switching from a generic ad blocker to LinkTopics

If you’re using a generic ad blocker on LinkedIn and still seeing Sponsored posts, Suggested Posts or “Liked by” clutter, you're not alone — and you're not doing anything wrong.

This guide shows exactly how to switch to LinkTopics for cleaner, more precise filtering designed specifically for LinkedIn.

1. Understand what your ad blocker does — and what it can’t

Generic ad blockers like AdBlock, uBlock or AdGuard are fantastic for the general web. But LinkedIn’s feed does not use traditional ad formats. Instead, it uses:

  • Native Sponsored posts embedded inside the feed
  • Suggested Posts disguised as recommendations
  • Reaction-based “Liked by” posts
  • Dynamic components that change frequently

These are not classic banners — so a generic “linkedin adblock” approach often fails.

For a full breakdown of the types of noise inside LinkedIn, you can also read: Remove ads and noise on LinkedIn

2. Install LinkTopics alongside your current blocker

You don’t need to uninstall anything. Just:

  1. Install LinkTopics from the Chrome Web Store
  2. Open LinkedIn
  3. Refresh the page so LinkTopics can start filtering

Your existing blocker will continue handling banners and trackers on other sites. LinkTopics takes care of LinkedIn’s internal feed noise.

3. Enable the filters your ad blocker misses

Inside the LinkTopics popup, toggle on:

  • Hide Sponsored / Promoted posts
  • Hide Suggested Posts
  • Hide “Liked by” / reaction-based posts

This removes the majority of distractions that generic blockers cannot detect.

You can also explore how LinkTopics removes “Liked by” clutter: Why “Liked by” posts clutter your feed

4. Test for one week and adjust

Use LinkedIn normally for a few days and notice:

  • How much less scrolling you do
  • How much cleaner your feed feels
  • Whether you want to keep or disable certain toggles

You can pause LinkTopics anytime from the popup without removing it.

5. Enjoy a cleaner, more predictable LinkedIn

You now have a two-layer setup:

  • Your ad blocker for the rest of the web
  • LinkTopics for LinkedIn's internal feed clutter

This gives you the best of both worlds — without breaking the UI or missing important posts.

Final step: complete your setup

If LinkedIn is part of your daily work — sales, recruiting, learning, networking — switching to a purpose-built tool makes a noticeable difference in focus and productivity.
