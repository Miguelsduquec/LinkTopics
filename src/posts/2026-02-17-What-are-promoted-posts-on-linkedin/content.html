What Are Promoted Posts on LinkedIn and Why You See So Many

How promoted posts work, why they blend in, and what control users actually have.

If you scroll through LinkedIn regularly, you’ve likely noticed posts that feel like recommendations — but aren’t.

They don’t come from people you follow, yet they appear naturally in your feed.

These are promoted posts.

What Exactly Is a Promoted Post?

A promoted post is paid content distributed directly into the LinkedIn feed.

Unlike traditional ads, promoted posts:

  • use the same layout as organic posts,
  • appear between regular updates,
  • are labeled subtly as “Promoted”.

Why Promoted Posts Blend Into the Feed

LinkedIn intentionally minimizes the visual difference between promoted and organic posts.

This allows promoted content to:

  • benefit from normal scrolling behavior,
  • feel like recommendations instead of ads,
  • capture attention without interruption.

Why You’re Seeing More Promoted Posts Than Before

The growth of promoted posts is driven by:

  • increased advertiser demand,
  • strong performance of feed-based ads,
  • longer average LinkedIn sessions.

As LinkedIn grows, promoted content scales with it.

Why Manual Hiding Doesn’t Work Long Term

Each promoted post belongs to a different campaign.

Hiding one does not reduce the next.
