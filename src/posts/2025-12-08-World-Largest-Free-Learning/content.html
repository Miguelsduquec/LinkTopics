Why Your LinkedIn Feed Feels Broken

LinkedIn is trying to do several things at once:

  • keep you on the platform longer,
  • boost posts that create engagement,
  • serve ads and promoted posts,
  • and (sometimes) show posts from people you actually care about.

In practice, this means your feed mixes:

  • posts from people you follow,
  • posts your connections liked or commented on,
  • Suggested Posts based on topics and trends,
  • promoted posts and ads.

When all of that gets blended together, your feed can start to feel noisy and off-topic — especially if you use LinkedIn for focused professional work.

How the LinkedIn Algorithm Works (In Simple Terms)

The algorithm looks at signals like:

  • Who you interact with (likes, comments, shares, DMs),
  • What topics you engage with,
  • What keeps you scrolling (dwell time),
  • What’s performing globally (viral content),
  • Which ads target your profile.

The more you engage with a type of content, the more LinkedIn assumes you want it.

Accidentally engage with low-value posts or drama threads? Your feed can quickly tilt in the wrong direction.

Can You “Reset” the LinkedIn Algorithm?

There is no official reset feed button.

But you can influence your feed by:

  • cleaning up who you follow,
  • stopping engagement with content you don’t want,
  • using LinkedIn’s native controls,
  • and filtering the feed in your browser.

Stop feeding the algorithm bad signals — and start sending the right ones.

Step 1 — Unfollow the Right People (Without Disconnecting)

Unfollowing is one of the fastest wins. You stay connected and can still message — you just stop seeing their posts.

Start with accounts that repeatedly post:

  • drama or outrage bait,
  • vague motivation and “inspirational” spam,
  • irrelevant content for your goals.

Step 2 — Hide Posts You Don’t Want More Of

When you see a post that shouldn’t be in your feed, use the three-dot menu and choose:

  • “I don’t want to see this”, or
  • “Show less posts like this” (if available).

Over time, these signals add up.

Step 3 — Be Intentional With Engagement

Every like and comment is basically a vote.

If you want a more professional feed, engage more with:

  • industry deep dives,
  • practical career insights,
  • case studies and breakdowns,
  • technical content that helps you perform better.

If you want a simple routine around this, read: Why LinkedIn is a productivity goldmine (if used right).

Step 4 — Clean Up Notifications and Topics

Notification overload pulls you into content you don’t actually care about.

  • Disable non-essential notifications.
  • Unfollow irrelevant hashtags and topics.
  • Unsubscribe from emails you never open.

The fewer distractions you allow, the easier it is to use LinkedIn intentionally.
