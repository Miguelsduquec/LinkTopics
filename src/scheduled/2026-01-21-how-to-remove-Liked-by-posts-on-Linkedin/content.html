Why You’re Seeing “Liked by” Posts on LinkedIn (2026)

LinkedIn’s algorithm prioritizes engagement, not pure relevance. When someone in your network likes a post, LinkedIn assumes:

“If your connection liked it, maybe you will too.”

Some reasons LinkedIn keeps showing these posts:

  • LinkedIn wants more engagement and more time spent on the platform.
  • It boosts viral content and increases reach.
  • It fills your feed when your follow list is small or inactive.
  • It promotes “popular” content even when it doesn’t match your career goals.

The result is familiar: you open LinkedIn and see posts you didn’t choose — framed as “Liked by João” / “Reacted by Maria” / “Commented by Ana”.

Can You Disable “Liked by” Posts on LinkedIn?

No. LinkedIn does not offer a native setting to disable “Liked by” / “Reacted by” posts.

Not in Feed Preferences. Not in Privacy. Not in any hidden menu.

You can hide posts one by one, or unfollow people… but that doesn’t solve the core issue.

So what can you actually do? There are three options.

Option 1 — Reduce “Liked by” Posts With Manual Actions

This helps a little, but won’t remove them completely.

1) Hide individual posts

Click the “…” menu on a post and select “I don’t want to see this” (or a similar option).

2) Use “Show less” when available

If LinkedIn offers “Show less posts like this”, use it consistently.

3) Unfollow people who constantly amplify low-value content

Unfollowing isn’t rude — it’s feed hygiene.

These steps take time, and LinkedIn often reintroduces the same patterns anyway.
