Why You’re Seeing “Liked by” Posts on LinkedIn (2026)

LinkedIn’s algorithm prioritizes engagement, not relevance. When someone in your network likes a post, LinkedIn assumes:

“If your friend liked it, maybe you will too.”

But this logic rarely works in real life.

Some reasons LinkedIn keeps showing these posts:

  • LinkedIn wants more engagement and more time spent on the platform.
  • It helps boost viral content and reach.
  • It fills your feed when you don’t follow many people.
  • It pushes “popular” content even if it’s irrelevant to your career.

So the result?

You open LinkedIn and see:

  • Posts about industries you don’t care about.
  • Content from people you don’t know.

Can You Disable “Liked by” Posts on LinkedIn?

Short answer: No — LinkedIn doesn’t offer a setting for this.

Not in the Feed Preferences. Not in the Privacy settings. Not in any hidden menu.

You can manually hide posts one by one… but that doesn't fix the feed.

You can unfollow people… but LinkedIn still shows liked posts from your extended network.

So what can you actually do?

There are three options.

Option 1 — Reduce “Liked by” Posts With Manual Settings

This helps a little, but won’t remove them completely.

1. Hide individual posts

Click the “…” on a post and select “I don't want to see this”.

2. Tell LinkedIn what you don’t like

Use the “Show less posts like this” option whenever available.

3. Unfollow people who only engage with low-quality content

Harsh… but effective in some cases.

4. Turn off some types of notifications

Reducing notification noise can slightly improve what appears in your feed.

These steps take time. And LinkedIn usually brings the posts back later anyway.
