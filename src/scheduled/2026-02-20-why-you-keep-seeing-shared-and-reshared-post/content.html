Why You Keep Seeing Shared and Reshared Posts on LinkedIn

Why the same content keeps resurfacing in your feed — and what control you realistically have.

If your LinkedIn feed feels repetitive, shared and reshared posts are often the reason.

You might scroll past the same idea several times — shared by different people, with little added context.

This repetition is not accidental. It’s a core part of how LinkedIn distributes content.

How Shared Posts Spread Across the Network

When someone shares a post, LinkedIn treats it as fresh network activity.

That activity can push the same post into multiple feeds, even if the original content is unchanged.

The more people who reshare it, the more visibility it gains — regardless of whether it’s still useful to you.

Why Repetition Hurts Feed Quality

Reshared posts crowd out original updates.

Instead of seeing new ideas or perspectives, your feed fills with:

  • the same post reframed multiple times,
  • identical opinions repeated by different voices,
  • content optimized for reach rather than relevance.

Over time, this makes the feed feel noisier and less intentional.

The Network Effect You Can’t Fully Control

Even if you curate who you follow, resharing bypasses that control.

Posts spread because of:

  • network connections,
  • social proof,
  • visibility boosts from repeated sharing.

This is why feed control often feels incomplete, as discussed in: Your Feed Is Your Competitive Advantage

What LinkedIn Lets You Do About Reshared Posts

LinkedIn offers a few manual controls:

  • hide individual shared posts,
  • unfollow people who reshare frequently,
  • limit engagement with repetitive content.

LinkedIn explains hiding and muting options here: LinkedIn Help — Hide and mute feed content

These actions help, but they require constant maintenance.

Why Manual Cleanup Doesn’t Scale

Reshared posts are generated by ongoing network behavior.

As long as people keep sharing, repetition continues — even if you hide individual items.
