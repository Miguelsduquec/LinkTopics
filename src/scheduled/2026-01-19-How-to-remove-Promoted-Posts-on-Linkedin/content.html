What Are Promoted Posts on LinkedIn?

Promoted posts are paid ads that appear in your feed and look almost exactly like normal posts. They usually include a small label such as “Promoted” or “Sponsored”.

Advertisers pay LinkedIn to show these posts to you, even if:

  • you don’t follow the company,
  • you’ve never interacted with their content,
  • you’re not interested in their product at all.

From LinkedIn’s point of view, this is normal. Ads fund the platform. But from your point of view as a user, these promoted posts:

  • break the flow of useful content,
  • push irrelevant offers and products,
  • make your feed feel noisy and unprofessional.

Why You’re Seeing More Promoted Posts in 2026

There are three core reasons why promoted posts feel more aggressive than ever in 2026:

  1. Revenue pressure. Like every big platform, LinkedIn is under pressure to grow ad revenue. That means more sponsored slots in your feed.
  2. Better targeting. The more data LinkedIn has, the more it can sell targeted ads. That leads to a higher volume of promoted posts.
  3. Blended design. Promoted posts are now designed to look almost identical to organic posts, which makes them feel more intrusive.

The result: even if you use LinkedIn for serious, professional reasons, you still get a constant stream of ads and sponsored messages.

Can You Hide Promoted Posts on LinkedIn Using Settings?

LinkedIn does not offer a toggle like “Disable promoted posts” or “Remove sponsored content”.

However, you can reduce the noise a little by using the native controls:

1. Hide individual ads

On any promoted post, click the three-dot menu and choose “I don’t want to see this ad”. You can sometimes specify a reason, such as “Irrelevant” or “Repetitive”.

2. Adjust ad preferences

Go to Settings > Advertising and review your ad preferences. You can disable certain categories of targeted ads or reduce how your data is used for advertising.

3. Unfollow noisy company pages

Some promoted content comes from companies you already follow. Unfollowing them may reduce some sponsored posts.

These steps help a bit, but they will never remove all promoted posts. LinkedIn will always find new ways to show you ads.

  • Installing a browser-wide adblocker (AdBlock, uBlock Origin, etc.),

  • Using filters meant for other websites.

Sometimes these tools can block obvious banner ads. But LinkedIn’s promoted posts are different:

  • They are rendered as feed elements, not separate ad frames.
  • They use dynamic classes and markup that can change frequently.
  • They mimic organic posts visually, making them harder to target with simple rules.
