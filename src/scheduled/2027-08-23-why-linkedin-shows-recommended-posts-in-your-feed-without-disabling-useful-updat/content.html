Why LinkedIn Shows Recommended Posts in Your Feed That Actually Matters

LinkedIn increasingly pushes recommendations into the feed. Here is how to understand suggested and recommended posts and reduce them when they stop being useful.

If you are searching for why LinkedIn shows recommended posts, you are probably trying to get back to a feed led by your network instead of algorithmic insertions.

Quick Answer

Quick answer: LinkedIn does not give you a native global option to turn off recommended posts. The platform keeps reinserting them, so manual cleanup helps only briefly.

Common Labels You May See in the Feed

  • Suggested
  • Suggested for you
  • Recommended
  • Because you follow

These labels often appear in the small context line above the main post card, which is the part many people want to remove from the feed first.

Key Takeaways

  • Recommended posts are designed to increase discovery and engagement.
  • LinkedIn does not offer a native global switch to turn them off.
  • These cards often crowd out updates from the people and companies you follow.
  • A LinkedIn-focused filter is the fastest way to reduce them consistently.

Why LinkedIn Keeps Showing Recommended Posts

LinkedIn surfaces recommended posts to increase discovery and keep the feed feeling active. Instead of showing only updates from people and companies you follow, the algorithm adds posts it believes might hold your attention.

LinkedIn pushes more than just posts from people you follow. It also injects promoted content, social activity, recommendations, and repeated distribution patterns that make the feed harder to use intentionally.

For people who rely on LinkedIn for recruiting, sales, research, hiring, or professional visibility, this extra noise adds up quickly.

Why This Pattern Becomes a Feed Problem

  • It pushes relevant updates further down the feed.
  • It increases scrolling friction and weakens focus.
  • It rewards engagement patterns more than professional relevance.
  • It makes LinkedIn feel busier without making it more useful.

This is one reason users search for terms such as why LinkedIn shows recommended posts rather than generic social media advice.

Why Native Controls Usually Fall Short

LinkedIn lets you hide specific posts and adjust some feed preferences, but those controls do not remove recommended posts globally. In practice, manual cleanup helps for a moment and then the same pattern returns in a slightly different form.

That is why manual cleanup feels repetitive: the same type of distraction keeps reappearing in slightly different forms.

  • Preserve useful content from your real network.
  • Lower the amount of feed friction you deal with every day.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can I turn off Suggested Posts on LinkedIn?

No. LinkedIn does not offer a global setting to disable Suggested or Recommended posts in the feed.

Why does LinkedIn keep recommending posts I never asked to see?

The platform uses discovery and engagement goals to fill the feed with content it believes may keep you scrolling, even when it is only loosely relevant.

Are Suggested Posts the same as Sponsored Posts?

No. Sponsored posts are paid placements, while Suggested posts are algorithmic recommendations inserted into the feed.

What is the fastest way to reduce recommended posts?

The simplest approach is to use a LinkedIn-focused filtering extension that can identify Suggested and Recommended cards directly in the feed and hide them automatically.
