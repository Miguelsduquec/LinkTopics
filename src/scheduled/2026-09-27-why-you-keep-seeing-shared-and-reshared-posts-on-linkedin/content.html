Why LinkedIn Keeps Showing Reshared Posts

LinkedIn amplifies reshared posts because reposts and shares extend distribution without requiring original content. This helps the platform keep the feed active, even when the content itself is not directly relevant to you.

LinkedIn pushes more than just posts from people you follow. It also injects promoted content, social activity, recommendations, and repeated distribution patterns that make the feed harder to use intentionally.

For people who rely on LinkedIn for recruiting, sales, research, hiring, or professional visibility, this extra noise adds up quickly.

Why This Pattern Becomes a Feed Problem

  • It pushes relevant updates further down the feed.
  • It increases scrolling friction and weakens focus.
  • It rewards engagement patterns more than professional relevance.
  • It makes LinkedIn feel busier without making it more useful.

This is one reason users search for terms such as hide reshared posts on LinkedIn rather than generic social media advice.

Why Native Controls Usually Fall Short

LinkedIn lets you hide specific posts and adjust some feed preferences, but those controls do not remove reshared posts globally. In practice, manual cleanup helps for a moment and then the same pattern returns in a slightly different form.

That is why manual cleanup feels repetitive: the same type of distraction keeps reappearing in slightly different forms.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can I turn off reposted or reshared posts on LinkedIn?

Not globally. LinkedIn lets you hide individual items, but there is no built-in toggle to disable shared or reposted posts as a category.

Why do reposted posts feel so repetitive on LinkedIn?

Because the same content can be distributed repeatedly through different people in your network, which makes the feed feel more repetitive than informative.

Will muting one connection solve the repost problem?

Usually not. Reposted content often comes through many people, so muting one source rarely removes the underlying pattern.

What is the easiest way to reduce reshared posts?

A LinkedIn-specific feed filter can hide shared and reposted cards automatically, which is more reliable than manually hiding them every time they appear.
