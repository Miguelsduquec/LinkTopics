Adblock Plus for LinkedIn: Why Generic Ad Blockers Don’t Clean Your Feed

Updated for 2026 — why blocking ads helps, but doesn’t fix LinkedIn feed relevance.

Adblock Plus is one of the most widely used ad blockers in the world. Naturally, many professionals install it hoping to clean up their LinkedIn experience.

At first, it works — some ads disappear.

But then a familiar frustration returns:

“Why is my LinkedIn feed still full of irrelevant posts?”

If you feel that, you’re not alone. The real issue is that LinkedIn’s noise isn’t just advertising — it’s the structure of the feed.

What Is Adblock Plus?

Adblock Plus is a browser extension designed to block ads, trackers, and intrusive elements across the web.

It relies on filter lists and rules that identify known advertising patterns.

You can read more about how it works on the official website: adblockplus.org

Because LinkedIn serves sponsored content like most websites, Adblock Plus can hide many obvious ad formats.

What Adblock Plus Does Well on LinkedIn

  • Blocks many sponsored elements (banner-style ads and some promoted blocks).
  • Easy to install for non-technical users.
  • Works across websites — not LinkedIn-only.
  • Reduces visual clutter caused by ads.

If ads are your main pain point, this alone can make LinkedIn feel less commercial.

The Problem With Generic Ad Blockers on LinkedIn

LinkedIn is not just a website — it’s a social feed driven by behavior.

Even with ads reduced, your feed still includes:

  • posts liked or commented on by your connections,
  • “Liked by” / “Reacted by” posts from people you don’t follow,
  • suggested posts from strangers,
  • algorithmic recommendations based on engagement,
  • viral and trending content.

These elements are not traditional ads, so ad blockers usually ignore them.

If you’re trying to remove “Liked by” posts specifically, this is the most direct guide: How to Remove “Liked by” Posts on LinkedIn (2026 Guide).

Limitations of Using Adblock Plus on LinkedIn

  • No understanding of feed context (it doesn’t know what’s relevant to your career).
  • Cannot reliably remove “Liked by” / “Reacted by” posts.
  • No topic filtering (keep “tech”, hide “sales”, etc.).
  • No control over suggested content.
  • No visibility — you don’t get counters or clarity about what changed.

Adblock Plus treats LinkedIn like a static page — not a dynamic, algorithm-driven feed.

Adblock Plus vs LinkedIn Feed Cleaners

Ad blockers focus on blocking ads.

Feed cleaners focus on improving relevance and removing noisy post types.
