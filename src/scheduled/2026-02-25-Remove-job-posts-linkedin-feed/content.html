Why Job Posts Appear in Your LinkedIn Feed (And How to Reduce Them)

Why job-related content shows up outside the Jobs tab — and what control you realistically have.

LinkedIn has a dedicated Jobs tab — yet job posts still appear regularly in the main feed.

For users not actively job hunting, this content often feels misplaced and disruptive.

The reason is tied to how LinkedIn distributes engagement-driven content.

Why Job Posts Are Treated Like Feed Content

Job posts are not just listings. They are engagement units.

When someone:

  • shares a role,
  • likes a job post,
  • comments on a hiring update,

LinkedIn may surface that activity in the feed.

Why Job Posts Often Feel Irrelevant

Feed-based job distribution ignores intent.

You may see roles because:

  • someone in your network engaged with them,
  • they match a past title,
  • they are being promoted broadly.

This leads to job content appearing even when you’re not looking.

The Cost of Job Noise in the Feed

For professionals using LinkedIn to learn, sell, or build visibility, job posts create friction.

Common effects include:

  • less room for relevant updates,
  • more scrolling to find signal,
  • a fragmented feed experience.

This reinforces the idea that attention is shaped by what the feed prioritizes: Your Feed Is Your Competitive Advantage

What LinkedIn Lets You Do

LinkedIn provides limited manual controls:

  • hide individual job-related posts,
  • reduce engagement with job content,
  • adjust job alert preferences.

LinkedIn documents content hiding here: LinkedIn Help — Hide and mute feed content

These actions help, but they don’t keep job posts out permanently.

Why Manual Control Doesn’t Scale

As long as your network engages with job posts, they keep appearing.

This creates the same scalability problem described here: How to Clean Your LinkedIn Feed Manually (And Why It Doesn’t Scale)

Final Thoughts

Job posts serve a purpose, but not in every feed.

Once they start displacing relevant content, they become noise.

If you want a feed aligned with your current goals, separating jobs from content is a key step.
