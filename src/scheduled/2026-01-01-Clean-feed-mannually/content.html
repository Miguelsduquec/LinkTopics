How to Clean Your LinkedIn Feed Manually (And Why It Doesn’t Scale)

Updated for 2026 — what manual LinkedIn feed cleanup gets right, and where it breaks down.

Many professionals try to clean their LinkedIn feed without using any tools.

At first, this approach feels responsible and effective. You unfollow a few accounts, hide some posts, and the feed improves.

But then, slowly, the noise comes back.

This article explains why.

Why People Try to Clean Their LinkedIn Feed Manually

Manual cleanup feels like the “correct” solution:

  • no extensions,
  • no third-party tools,
  • just using LinkedIn as intended.

LinkedIn itself encourages this behavior through options like unfollowing, hiding posts, and muting topics.

What Manual LinkedIn Feed Cleanup Actually Involves

Cleaning your feed manually usually means doing some or all of the following:

  • unfollowing people while staying connected,
  • hiding posts you don’t want to see again,
  • muting hashtags and topics,
  • reducing notifications,
  • engaging only with content you like.

LinkedIn explains some of these controls in their official help documentation: LinkedIn Help — Unfollow, mute, and hide posts

What Works Well With Manual Cleanup

  • Unfollowing noisy accounts reduces repeated low-value posts.
  • Hiding irrelevant posts sends better signals to the algorithm.
  • Intentional engagement helps shape what you see over time.

In the short term, this can noticeably improve your feed.

Why Manual LinkedIn Feed Cleanup Doesn’t Scale

The core problem is simple:

The LinkedIn algorithm never stops.

Even after careful cleanup, your feed continues to include:

  • posts liked or commented on by your connections,
  • suggested posts from outside your network,
  • engagement-driven or viral content,
  • promoted posts and subtle ads.

Each new interaction from your network reintroduces noise.

The Hidden Cost of Manual Cleanup

Manual feed cleanup also has hidden downsides:

  • It requires constant attention.
  • You need to make decisions on every scroll.
  • The results are inconsistent.
  • New noise appears faster than you can remove it.

Over time, this leads to frustration and burnout.

Manual Cleanup vs Understanding the Algorithm

Manual actions help, but they don’t override the system.

If you want to understand why LinkedIn behaves this way, read our guide on how the LinkedIn algorithm works in 2026 .

The takeaway is clear: the algorithm optimizes for engagement, not focus.

When Manual Cleanup Is Not Enough

Manual cleanup breaks down if you:

  • use LinkedIn daily,
  • follow hundreds or thousands of connections,
  • work in fast-moving industries,
  • value focus and relevance.

At that point, the effort required outweighs the benefit.
