Comments Beat Posts: The Fastest Way to Grow on LinkedIn Without Posting

If you don’t want to be a full-time creator, you don’t have to. Strategic commenting is one of the highest-ROI ways to build visibility on LinkedIn.

Why Posting Isn’t the Only Path

A lot of people treat LinkedIn growth like it’s “post daily or stay invisible.” That’s not true.

Posting is powerful, but it’s also time-consuming. If you don’t have a content pipeline, consistency becomes stressful — and stress kills momentum.

Why Comments Create Faster Visibility

Comments work because they show up where attention already exists.

  • You borrow distribution: Instead of building your own audience from scratch, you show up inside existing ones.
  • You build familiarity: People see your name repeatedly next to useful ideas.
  • You start conversations: The author often replies. Others jump in. DMs follow naturally.

And unlike posting, you can do this in 10 minutes a day.

The 10-Minute Commenting System

Here’s a simple routine that works:

  • Minute 1–2: Open LinkedIn with a goal (don’t scroll “just to check”).
  • Minute 3–7: Leave 2–3 thoughtful comments on posts from people your audience follows.
  • Minute 8–10: Reply to anyone who responds and continue the thread.

That’s enough to build recognition — without becoming a creator.

What a “Good Comment” Looks Like

A good comment is not “Great post!”

Use one of these formats:

  • Add a missing step: “One thing I’d add here is…”
  • Share a micro-example: “We tried this and saw…”
  • Ask a smart question: “How do you handle X when Y happens?”

Your goal is to be useful, not loud.

Protect Your Attention (This Is the Hidden Edge)

The hardest part of “commenting daily” isn’t writing comments — it’s avoiding the noise that drags you into endless scrolling.

If your feed is full of ads, “Liked by” clutter and random suggested content, staying consistent becomes way harder.
