Why LinkedIn Keeps Showing “Likes This” Posts

LinkedIn boosts updates marked with labels like “likes this” because engagement is treated as a relevance signal. When someone in your network reacts to a post, LinkedIn assumes that social proof is enough reason to show it to you too.

LinkedIn pushes more than just posts from people you follow. It also injects promoted content, social activity, recommendations, and repeated distribution patterns that make the feed harder to use intentionally.

For people who rely on LinkedIn for recruiting, sales, research, hiring, or professional visibility, this extra noise adds up quickly.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can I disable 'likes this' or 'celebrates this' posts on LinkedIn?

LinkedIn does not provide a native setting to disable reaction-driven posts globally. You can hide them one by one, but the pattern keeps coming back.

Why does LinkedIn show me posts just because someone reacted to them?

LinkedIn treats reactions as engagement signals and uses them to expand distribution. That is why a single reaction can pull an unrelated post into your feed.

Are 'loves this' and 'celebrates this' posts different from 'likes this' posts?

They are variations of the same feed pattern: social activity from another person or page is used as the reason to show you the underlying post.

What is the easiest way to remove “likes this” posts?

A LinkedIn-specific extension such as LinkTopics can recognise “likes this” posts in the feed and hide them automatically, which is much faster than hiding them manually one by one.
