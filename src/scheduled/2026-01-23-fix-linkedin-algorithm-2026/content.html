Why Your LinkedIn Feed Feels Broken

The problem isn’t that the algorithm is “bad”. It’s that LinkedIn is trying to do multiple things at once:

  • keep you on the platform longer,
  • show content that generates engagement,
  • serve ads and promoted posts,
  • and still surface posts from people you actually follow.

In practice, your feed becomes a blend of:

  • posts from people you follow,
  • posts they liked or commented on (“Liked by” / “Reacted by”),
  • suggested posts based on trends and topics,
  • promoted posts and ads.

When all of that is mixed together, your feed can feel noisy and off-topic — especially if you use LinkedIn for focused, professional reasons.

How the LinkedIn Algorithm Works in 2026 (Simplified)

You don’t need every technical detail. But understanding the signals helps you influence what you see.

In simple terms, LinkedIn looks at:

  • Who you interact with (likes, comments, shares, DMs),
  • What topics you engage with (tech, HR, sales, AI, etc.),
  • What keeps you scrolling (dwell time / time spent),
  • What’s performing well globally (viral content),
  • What’s promoted (ads targeting your profile).

The more you engage with a content type, the more the algorithm assumes you want similar posts.

That can work in your favor — or backfire fast.

Can You “Reset” the LinkedIn Algorithm?

There is no official “reset feed” button on LinkedIn.

But you can do something more practical: stop feeding the algorithm the wrong signals and start sending the right ones.

Here’s the plan.

Step 1 — Unfollow the Right People (Without Disconnecting)

One of the fastest improvements is to unfollow accounts that constantly amplify low-value content.

Unfollow ≠ disconnect. You stay connected, you just stop seeing their posts in your feed.

Start with:

  • people who constantly engage with drama,
  • accounts that share vague motivational posts every day,
  • company pages that aren’t relevant to your direction.

Step 2 — Hide Posts You Don’t Want More Of

Every time you see a post and think “Why is this here?”, use the three-dot menu and choose:

  • “I don’t want to see this”, or
  • “Show less posts like this” (if available).

Small actions compound over time.

Step 3 — Engage Intentionally (Your Engagement Is a Vote)

Every like/comment/share tells LinkedIn:

“More content like this, please.”

If you engage with outrage threads, pointless debates, or clickbait, your feed will drift in that direction.

Instead, engage with:

  • industry deep dives,
  • case studies and breakdowns,
  • technical content that improves your work,
  • high-signal creators in your niche.

Step 4 — Reduce Notification Noise

Notification overload pulls you into content you wouldn’t choose intentionally.

  • Turn off non-essential notifications.
  • Stop following irrelevant hashtags/topics.
  • Unsubscribe from emails you never read.

The calmer your environment, the easier it is to use LinkedIn strategically.

FAQ — Fixing the LinkedIn Algorithm

How do I fix my LinkedIn algorithm in 2026?

Unfollow noisy profiles, hide irrelevant posts, engage intentionally, and use a feed cleaner like LinkTopics if you want stronger control over clutter and promoted content.

Why is my LinkedIn feed full of random posts?

Because LinkedIn boosts what your network engages with, mixes in suggested posts, and inserts promoted content. That blend can feel chaotic.

Can I reset my LinkedIn feed?

No official reset button exists. But you can “soft reset” your feed by unfollowing, hiding posts, and changing your engagement signals. A feed filter gives you extra control.

Is there a way to only see relevant posts on LinkedIn?

You can get much closer by curating your follows and engagement — and by filtering out clutter patterns like “Liked by”, suggested, and promoted blocks.

What is the best way to clean my LinkedIn feed?

Combine native actions (unfollow + hide + intentional engagement) with a dedicated feed cleaner if you want a more consistent result.
