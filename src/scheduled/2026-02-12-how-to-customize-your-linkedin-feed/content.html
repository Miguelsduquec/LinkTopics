How to Customize Your LinkedIn Feed (Settings You Didn’t Know)

Updated for 2026 — the LinkedIn feed controls most people never use, and why they only go so far.

If your LinkedIn feed feels random, noisy or exhausting, you might assume there’s nothing you can do about it.

But LinkedIn actually offers more customization options than most users realize.

The problem?

They’re scattered, limited, and easy to overlook.

Why LinkedIn Feed Customization Feels Hidden

LinkedIn doesn’t present feed controls as a single dashboard.

Instead, customization happens through:

  • small actions inside posts,
  • settings buried in menus,
  • behavior-based signals.

This makes feed customization feel unintuitive — even though it exists.

1. Unfollow Without Disconnecting

One of the most powerful LinkedIn feed settings is also the least used: Unfollow.

Unfollowing someone:

  • keeps the connection,
  • keeps DMs open,
  • removes their posts from your feed.

This is ideal for noisy posters you don’t want to offend.

2. Hide Posts and Train the Algorithm

Every post menu includes options like:

  • “I don’t want to see this”,
  • “Show less posts like this”.

Using these options sends negative signals to the algorithm.

LinkedIn explains this behavior-based filtering in their help center: LinkedIn Help — Hide and mute feed content

3. Mute Topics and Hashtags

Following hashtags influences your feed more than most users realize.

If your feed is full of trends you don’t care about, check:

  • which hashtags you follow,
  • which topics you’ve interacted with historically.

Unfollowing or muting irrelevant hashtags reduces repeated noise.

4. Control Notifications to Control the Feed

Notifications drive engagement — and engagement drives feed content.

If you’re constantly pulled into:

  • reaction notifications,
  • comment threads,
  • random updates,

…the algorithm assumes you want more of that content.

Reducing notifications helps reduce feed chaos.

5. Engage Intentionally (This Matters More Than You Think)

Every like, comment or share is a vote.

If you engage with:

  • outrage posts,
  • low-effort content,
  • clickbait discussions,

LinkedIn will show you more of the same.

The Hard Truth: Native Settings Have Limits

Even if you use every setting correctly, LinkedIn will still show:

  • “Liked by” and reaction-based posts,
  • suggested content from outside your network,
  • promoted posts and subtle ads.

This is because the feed is designed for engagement — not focus.

Why Customization Alone Doesn’t Scale

Manual customization requires:

  • constant attention,
  • ongoing decisions,
  • continuous re-training of the algorithm.

Over time, most users stop maintaining it.
