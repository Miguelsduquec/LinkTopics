No LinkedIn Ads: Does Blocking Ads Really Clean Your LinkedIn Feed?

Updated for 2026 — why removing ads helps, but doesn’t solve the real LinkedIn feed problem.

If LinkedIn feels overwhelming, ads are often the first thing people blame. It’s no surprise that tools like No LinkedIn Ads have become popular.

But once the ads are gone, many users still ask the same question:

“Why does my LinkedIn feed still feel noisy?”

In this article, we’ll look at what No LinkedIn Ads actually fixes — and what it doesn’t.

Why LinkedIn Ads Are Only Part of the Problem

LinkedIn ads are visible and easy to dislike. They interrupt your scroll and feel commercial.

But ads are just one layer of LinkedIn’s feed.

On a typical scroll session, your feed also includes:

  • posts liked or commented on by your connections,
  • suggested posts from people you don’t follow,
  • “Because you viewed…” recommendations,
  • viral or engagement-driven content.

Even with ads removed, these elements continue to shape your experience.

What Is No LinkedIn Ads?

No LinkedIn Ads is a browser extension built specifically to block sponsored and promoted content on LinkedIn.

Its goal is straightforward: remove ads and make LinkedIn feel less commercial.

Once installed, it runs automatically and hides many obvious ad formats.

What No LinkedIn Ads Does Well

  • Focused purpose — it targets ads and promoted posts.
  • Simple setup — install and forget.
  • Lightweight — minimal performance impact.
  • Immediate relief — fewer sponsored interruptions.

If ads are your main frustration, this can noticeably improve your experience.

Limitations of No LinkedIn Ads

Blocking ads does not equal cleaning the feed.

  • No control over algorithmic content.
  • Doesn’t remove “Liked by” or “Reacted by” posts.
  • Suggested posts remain visible.
  • No topic filtering.
  • No visibility into what’s being removed.

As a result, many users still see:

  • posts from strangers,
  • content boosted by engagement rather than relevance,
  • low-value posts that technically aren’t ads.

Why Ad Blockers Don’t Fix LinkedIn Feeds

Ad blockers treat LinkedIn like a website.

But LinkedIn is a behavior-driven feed.

The biggest sources of noise come from:

  • network activity,
  • engagement signals,
  • recommendation systems,
  • content trends.

Generic ad blocking tools simply aren’t designed to understand this context.
